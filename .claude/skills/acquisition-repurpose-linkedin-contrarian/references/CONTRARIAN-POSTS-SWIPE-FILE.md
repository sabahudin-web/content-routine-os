## How to Find Your Contrarian Angles

Ask yourself:
- What does everyone in my niche believe that I think is wrong?
- What's overhyped in my space right now?
- What "success advice" has actually backfired for me?
- What do clients do that secretly wastes their time or money?

The angle MUST come from your actual experience — not something you read. No lived proof = no post.

## The 9 Winning Patterns

### Pattern 1 — Insider Criticism
You use the thing you're criticizing. That's your credibility.

**Structure:**
```
[Tool/Trend] won't [promised outcome]
(Coming from a [Tool/Trend] power user)

[What it's actually good at]
→ X
→ Y
→ Z

[What it actually can't do]
→ A
→ B
→ C

[Your real take — one punchy line]
```

**Example template:** "[Popular thing] won't [what people think it does]. (Coming from someone who uses it daily)"

---

### Pattern 2 — Moral Outrage
Call out bad behavior you've seen. Name it. Tell them what to do instead.

**Structure:**
```
"[Bad behavior quote]"

No you didn't.

You [what they actually did].

Every week I see another post:
→ [Example]
→ [Example]
→ [Example]

[Direct statement about why it's wrong]
[What to do instead]
```

---

### Pattern 3 — Escalation
Start with a mild word. Escalate to the real verdict.

**Structure:**
```
[Common criticism] isn't [mild word].

It's [stronger word].

[Evidence 1]
[Evidence 2]

[Personal proof]
```

---

### Pattern 4 — Two-Sentence Manifesto
No setup. Just the take. Short enough to screenshot.

**Structure:**
```
Unpopular opinion:

[Permission-giving statement that validates what your audience secretly wants to do]
```

---

### Pattern 5 — Insider Test / Gatekeeping
Makes readers feel smart if they get it. Creates in-group / out-group.

**Structure:**
```
[How to spot a fake / real [expert type]]

[Quick test or observation]

[Playful roast or in-group reward]
```

---

### Pattern 6 — Use Enemy's Evidence Against Them
The organization promoting X just did Y, which contradicts X.

**Structure:**
```
Everyone says: "[Common belief]"

[Famous org / person]: *[Does the opposite]*

[Ironic observation about what this reveals]
```

---

### Pattern 7 — Comparison Flip
4 hours beats 14. Less beats more.

**Structure:**
```
The most [positive outcome] people I know do [less/smaller thing].
The most [negative outcome]? [More/bigger thing].

[Personal story of believing the wrong side]

[What you learned]
```

---

### Pattern 8 — Radical Simplification
The biggest lie is that this is complicated. It's not.

**Structure:**
```
The biggest lie in [your industry]:

[The complexity myth people sell]

Here's what actually matters:
• [Simple thing 1]
• [Simple thing 2]

That's it.
```

---

### Pattern 9 — Trivial Topic + Extreme Conviction
Something absurdly low-stakes, defended with maximum seriousness.

**Structure:**
```
I'm about to lose followers over this.

[Trivial opinion stated flatly]

[The defense — with more conviction than it deserves]

This is the hill I'll die on.
```
