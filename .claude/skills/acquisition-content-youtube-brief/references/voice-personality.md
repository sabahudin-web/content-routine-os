## Core Voice Identity

**Three words:** Raw. Practical. Real.

**The feeling he leaves:** *"This guy is actually doing the work. No bullshit. I can trust him."*

He's the war-born solopreneur who learned business on the streets before the boardroom. He doesn't teach theory — he shares what he's bled for. His voice carries the weight of someone who started with nothing, made every mistake, and is still figuring it out in public.

## Signature Phrases

Use naturally — never force them:
- "The thing is..."
- "Here's the deal"
- "Let me be real"
- "Look..."
- "LoL" (casual, not overused)
- "PERIOD" (for emphasis)
- "That's it." (after a simple explanation)
- "WHO CAREEEEES"
- "The question is..."
- "P.S." and "P.P.S." — signature ending move
- Bosnian expressions: "selling fog"
- "messy path" / "figure it out" / "the hard way"

---

## Sentence Rhythm

- Short punchy sentences. Then longer ones to explain.
- Fragments are fine. Intentional.
- Line breaks often — for rhythm and readability.
- → arrows for lists and steps (not always bullet points)
- Numbers in hooks when sharing real results

## Storytelling Structure (for Pillar 3 content)

1. **Start in the mess** — Drop into a moment of struggle or confusion
2. **Show the internal fight** — What he was thinking and feeling
3. **The shift** — What he realized or did differently
4. **The lesson** — Never preachy, always earned
5. **Invitation** — P.S. question or call to share their experience

**Recurring story arc:** War → Poverty → Streets → No father → Figuring it out alone → Side hustles → Failed attempts → LinkedIn discovery → Jasmin as mentor → Building the business → Still learning

## Hook Strategy (Context-Dependent)

| Content Type | Hook Style |
|--------------|------------|
| Client wins / Results | Numeric, specific: "I turned 5,000 connections into 140 buyers." |
| Personal story | Emotional, scene-setting: "Memo to myself, 2 years ago:" |
| Strong opinion | Contrarian, direct: "Everyone's obsessed with the new AI tool. Here's my unpopular opinion:" |
| Humor / Relatable | Observational, light: "Me: Let me create something exciting / Claude: Thinking... Thinking..." |
| News / Updates | Factual, timely: "Anthropic just dropped scheduled tasks for Claude Code." |
| Nostalgia / Reflection | Juxtaposition: contrast past and present |

**NEVER USE:**
- "Stop X / Start Y" format — he finds it cheap and overused
- "Most people think..."
- "47 times" and similar AI-generated tropes
- Corporate jargon: "leverage", "synergy", "unlock potential"
- Preachy or superior tone

## The Creator Voice Test (Run Before Finalizing Any Script)

1. Does this sound like Saba or a polished social media guru?
2. Is he sharing a real experience or generic advice?
3. Is there an opinion here that's actually his?
4. Would his past self find this useful?
5. Is this fog-free?

If it fails any of these, rewrite it.
