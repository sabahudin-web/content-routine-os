# How To Use The Funny Post Swipe File

**Last updated:** [your date]

The `funny-posts-swipe-file.md` is your pattern library for humor. Read it before writing funny posts. Not to copy — to borrow structure and calibrate your edge.

---

## Quick System (4 Steps)

### Step 1: Pick Your Humor Mode

**Mode A — Observation:** You noticed something funny. Just say the thing. Don't add a lesson.

**Mode B — Story:** Something embarrassing or absurd happened to you. Tell it honestly. The humor is in the truth.

Ask: What happened recently that made you laugh at yourself? What behavior in your industry looks ridiculous when you really think about it?

---

### Step 2: Choose a Pattern

Use the patterns from the swipe file as structural guides. The 6 patterns cover most situations:
- Absurd Math → for exaggerating relatable behavior
- Confession → for self-deprecating moments
- Dialogue → for showing how work-brain invades real life
- One-Liner Parody → for roasting industry clichés
- Insider Test → for in-group moments
- Third-Person Reveal → for dramatic / embarrassing stories

---

### Step 3: Inject Your Voice

Before writing, answer:
1. What's the thing I'm laughing at? (must be real — never manufactured outrage)
2. What's the punchline? (the absurd pivot, the irony, the reveal)
3. Does this sound like something I'd actually say out loud?

Write the post — short, punchy. The joke should land by the 4th sentence at the latest.

---

### Step 4: The Gut Test

Would you actually laugh at this? If yes, post it. If it feels clever but cold — it's not in voice. Try again.

**Length target:** Under 150 words. Funny posts lose momentum when they overstay their welcome.

---

## When Reading The Swipe File

Look for:
- How fast the post gets to the punchline
- The "unexpected" pivot — where does the humor actually land?
- How much setup is actually needed (usually less than you think)
- Whether the post feels honest or performed

Then forget the example and write from your actual situation.
